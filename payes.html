Payesh

Payesh is very popular Bengali dish of Kheer. Payas in Sanskrit means milk. This is a creamy rice pudding delicately flavored with cardamom and filled with nuts like badam, pistachios etc giving an amazing color with inclusion of saffron strands. Payesh is extremely a luscious dessert and can be eaten at anytime of the day. It is specially made during festivals, feast and occasions. Payesh is a very simple and easy to prepare dessert.

This dessert is a traditional south Asian sweet dish made by boiling of rice or broken wheat with milk and sugar flavored with cardamom powder and raisins, pistachios and almonds added to give an excellent nutty flavor and taste. The dessert gives a pleasant lemon yellowish color by saffron strands. It is typically served as part of a meal or also consumed as a standalone dish. It is a very well known dish both during the Hindu and Muslim festivals. The word kheer is commonly used in Northern India, Pakistan and Nepal. Payasam or Payasa is used in the Southern India whereas in the region of Bengal it is called payesh.

这种甜点是一种传统的南亚甜食，由大米或碎小麦、牛奶和糖煮沸而成，并添加小豆蔻粉、葡萄干、开心果和杏仁调味，赋予极佳的坚果风味和口感。藏红花丝使甜点呈现出宜人的柠檬黄色。它通常作为膳食的一部分，也可以作为独立的菜肴食用。这是印度教和穆斯林节日期间非常著名的菜肴。 kheer 这个词在印度北部、巴基斯坦和尼泊尔常用。 Payasam 或 Payasa 在印度南部使用，而在孟加拉地区则称为 payesh.

Payesh is constant stirring and adding the sugar after the rice is soft and tender. The ideal rice used for making the payesh is the small grained Indian variety or else you can also use the basmati rice though the texture and look will be little less authentic. Generally vermicelli is used for making the Kheer or payasam. A traditional Bengali meal ends with payesh followed by other sweets. Payas/ Payesh is also regarded as an auspicious food and generally associated with Annaprashana and birthday celebrations in a Bengali household. It is called Kheer in Bengali if milk is used in a significantly greater amount than rice. It is a tradition in Bangladesh to serve payesh on Eid days for Muslims.
