Noodles

Noodles are a type of food made from unleavened dough which is either rolled flat and cut, stretched, or extruded, into long strips or strings. Noodles are a staple food in many cultures (for example, Chinese noodles, Filipino noodles, Indonesian noodles, Japanese noodles, Korean noodles, Vietnamese noodles, and long and medium length Italian pasta) and made into a variety of shapes.

Noodles have been the staple foods for many Asian countries since ancient time. They can be made from wheat, rice and other raw materials such as buckwheat, and starches derived from potato, sweet potato and pulses. Wheat noodles are produced mainly from flour, water and salt, and the basic steps of noodles production include dough mixing, sheeting, combining of sheets, resting, rolling and cutting, followed by different processing treatments (drying, boiling, steaming, frying and freezing) or a combination of these.

Noodles are staple foods in many regions of the world. Their most likely origin is China, where 4000-year-old noodles were found at the Lajia archeological site. Noodles can be made from various raw materials, for example, from cereals and pseudocereals with wheat and rice being predominant. In Asia, wheat noodles are so popular that around 40% of wheat consumption are ascribed to noodles. Most noodle types share the common processing steps of mixing ingredients, kneading, rolling or sheeting the dough, and cutting into pieces. There are many types of noodles
