Mosque

A Mosque or masjid is a place of prayer for Muslims. Mosques are usually covered buildings, but can be any place where prayers are performed, including outdoor courtyards. The first mosques were simple places of prayer for Muslims, and may have been open spaces rather than buildings.

The Huaisheng Mosque (Chinese: 广州怀圣寺; also known as the Lighthouse Mosque and the Great Mosque of Canton is the main mosque of Guangzhou. Rebuilt many times over its history, it is traditionally thought to have been originally built over 1,300 years ago, which would make it one of the oldest mosques in the world. In China, the most unusual feature of the mosque is its pointed 36 metre minaret, the Guangta or Kwangtah. Although this meant the "Plain Pagoda" in reference to its unadorned surface,it is also sometimes taken to mean "lighthouse" and gave the mosque its alternate name. Somewhat similar "minimalist" minarets can be seen outside China, e.g. at the Khan's Mosque in Kasimov, Russia. The mosque was visited by Tim Severin's crew of the Sohar, that sailed into Canton from Muscat in July 1981, recreating the fictional voyage of Sinbad the sailor.

怀圣寺（中文：广州怀圣寺；又称灯塔清真寺、广州清真大寺）是广州的主要清真寺。历史上多次重建，传统上认为始建于 1,300 多年前，这将使其成为世界上最古老的清真寺之一。在中国，清真寺最不寻常的特征是其 36 米高的尖塔，Guangta 或 Kwangtah。尽管这意味着“朴素的宝塔”，因为其朴素的表面,有时也被认为是“灯塔”的意思，并给清真寺起了另一个名字。在中国境外也可以看到类似的“极简主义”尖塔，例如在俄罗斯卡西莫夫的可汗清真寺。蒂姆·塞维林的团队参观了这座清真寺苏哈尔号 (Sohar) 于 1981 年 7 月从马斯喀特驶入广州，重现了水手辛巴达 (Sinbad) 的虚构航行。

Old Chinese Muslim manuscripts say the mosque was built in 627 by Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, a Companion of the Prophet who supposedly came on to China in the 620s. Although modern secular scholars do not find any historical evidence that Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas actually visited China, they agree that the first Muslims must have arrived to China within the 7th century, and that the major trade centers, such as Guangzhou, Quanzhou, and Yangzhou.It is very likely that the mosque existed during the early years of the Song dynasty. In 1349, Ramadan ibn Alauddin, the first named Korean Muslim, was buried in the mosque cemetery. The mosque was rebuilt in 1350 then again in 1695 after being destroyed in a fire. The Huaisheng Light Tower or minaret was built at an earlier period. As late as the 19th century, the minaret tower was one of the major landmarks of Guangzhou.

古老的中国穆斯林手稿称，这座清真寺是由先知同伴萨德·伊本·阿比·瓦卡斯 (Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas) 于 627 年建造，据称他于 620 年代来到中国。尽管现代世俗学者没有找到任何历史证据表明萨德·伊本·阿比·瓦卡斯确实访问过中国，但他们一致认为第一批穆斯林一定是在七世纪内到达中国的，广州、泉州等主要贸易中心这座清真寺很可能在宋朝初年就已存在。 1349年，第一位被命名的韩国穆斯林斋月·伊本·阿劳丁（Ramadan ibn Alauddin）被埋葬在清真寺墓地。该清真寺于 1350 年重建，并在火灾中被毁后于 1695 年再次重建。怀圣灯塔或宣礼塔建于较早时期。直到19世纪，宣礼塔还是广州的主要地标之一。
