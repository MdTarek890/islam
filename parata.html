Parata

Paratha is a type of flaky, layered flatbread common throughout South Asia. They can be simple layered with your choice of fat (such as ghee, butter, or shortening) and rolled into a variety of shapes (such as square, round, or triangular) or they can even be stuffed or filled, such as in aloo paratha or Mughlai paratha.

Paratha is made from a simple dough of flour, salt, oil, and water. The dough is divided and each piece is rolled out and smeared with a generous amount of ghee, butter, or shortening, and sprinkled with flour to help provide definition between the layers. It’s then rolled or folded in a specific way depending on the end shape you want, and then given a final roll. The paratha is then cooked. First, the paratha is cooked part of the way in a dry pan to ensure the inner layers are cooked through. Then, ghee is added to the pan and the paratha is cooked in the ghee until golden-brown.

Paratha 是由面粉、盐、油和水组成的简单面团制成的。将面团分开，将每一块擀开，涂上大量的酥油、黄油或起酥油，并撒上面粉以帮助确定各层之间的轮廓。然后根据您想要的最终形状以特定方式卷起或折叠，然后进行最终卷。然后将帕拉塔煮熟。首先，将帕拉塔在干锅中煮熟，以确保内层煮熟。然后，将酥油加入锅中，将帕拉塔在酥油中煮至金黄色。

Paratha, at least to me, always seemed like such an important part of the Bangladeshi food I grew up with. It made appearances often, like at breakfast or at dinner with beef curry. While we often relied on the store-bought version for convenience, it was always the biggest treat when my grandmother would make her homemade paratha. Paratha comes in many different forms square, round, triangular, filled, made with different flours, and so on — and each culture and person has their own take. For this recipe, I took inspiration from the paratha my grandmother made and leaned on some of her techniques for making them at home.

至少对我来说，帕拉塔似乎一直是我成长过程中孟加拉国食物的重要组成部分。它经常出现，比如早餐或咖喱牛肉晚餐。虽然为了方便起见，我们经常依赖商店购买的版本，但当我的祖母自制帕拉塔时，这总是最大的享受。帕拉塔有多种不同的形式，方形、圆形、三角形、夹馅、用不同的面粉制成等等——每种文化和人都有自己的看法。对于这个食谱，我从祖母制作的帕拉塔中汲取灵感，并借鉴了她在家制作帕拉塔的一些技巧。
