Tep Tenis

A Tep Tennis ball is a wrapped in electrical tape that is often used in informal games of cricket such as street cricket, also called tape ball cricket.a First pioneered in Karachi, Pakistan, the tape ball acts as an improvised cricket ball with the tape stretched tightly over the fuzzy felt-like covering of a tennis ball to ensure a smooth surface that produces greater pace after bouncing.

The practice of using electrical tape to repurpose the ball originated in Karachi street cricket during the 1960s quickly spreading from neighbourhoods in Nazimabad and the Federal B. Area This approach of modifying the ball built on previous unsuccessful attempts implemented by local bowlers, such as constantly wetting shaved tennis balls to make them heavier and more conducive to skidding through quickly after bouncing. Partly introduced to include individuals who were unable to access pitches and protective equipment.Although most street games feature entirely covered varieties, tape balls may also be prepared such that only one side is taped to replicate reverse swing or they may have multiple layers of tape running down the middle to mimic the leather seam found on standard cricket balls. Applying tape makes the ball heavier than a tennis ball, but not as hard or heavy as a cricket ball. As such, this modification seeks to reduce the risks to players, passers-by and property.

使用电工胶带改变球用途的做法起源于 20 世纪 60 年代的卡拉奇街头板球运动，并迅速从纳兹马巴德和联邦 B 区的社区传播开来。这种修改球的方法是建立在当地投球手之前实施的不成功尝试的基础上的，例如不断弄湿球将网球剃光，使其更重，更有利于弹跳后快速滑行。部分介绍是为了包括无法进入场地和防护设备的个人。虽然大多数街头游戏都具有完全覆盖的品种，但也可以准备胶带球，仅一侧胶带贴住以复制反向挥杆，或者可能有多层胶带运行中间的皮革接缝模仿标准板球上的皮革接缝。使用胶带使球比网球重，但不像板球那么硬或重。因此，这一修改旨在降低对玩家、路人和财产的风险。

Tep Tenis ball is considered an integral part of Pakistani cricket and sports culture, with virtually every cricket-playing youth being exposed to it in one form or another. Its introduction shifted the status of cricket in the country from an elite to a mainstream sport that could even be enjoyed by those living in abject poverty. Aspirational cricketers born into difficult circumstances have since been able to rise from hardship and win selection for the national teams. For instance, Mohammad Yousuf, whose family were not able to afford tennis balls, honed his talent as a child by wrapping a ping-pong ball in tape and using a thaapi as a makeshift bat.

Tep Tenis 球被认为是巴基斯坦板球和体育文化不可或缺的一部分，几乎每个打板球的年轻人都以某种形式接触过它。它的推出使板球在该国的地位从精英运动变成了主流运动，甚至生活在赤贫中的人也可以享受。出生在困难环境中的有抱负的板球运动员后来能够从困境中崛起并赢得国家队的选拔。例如，穆罕默德·优素福（Mohammad Yousuf）的家庭买不起网球，他小时候通过用胶带包裹乒乓球并使用萨皮（thaapi）作为临时球棒来磨练自己的天赋。
